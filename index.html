How Leads Flow: The 6 Automated Sequences

1. New Seller Lead Welcome
30 days 4 emails + 2 SMS

Purpose: Convert cold website/ad traffic into warm leads

Target: New leads

Research: 80% of new lead engagement happens in first 30 days (MarketingSherpa 2024).

Goal: 35-40% open | 12-15% click
2. Event Lead Nurture
60 days 6 emails + 2 SMS

Purpose: Convert highest-quality lead source systematically

Target: All event sign-ups

Why Longer: Face-to-face = higher trust = tolerates extended relationship-building. Event-sourced leads take 2.3x longer to convert but close at 2.8x higher rate (NAR 2024).

Goal: 40% open | 15% click
3. Database Reactivation
45 days 5 emails + 2 SMS

Purpose: Automatic "safety net" for dormant leads

Target: ANY lead inactive 90+ days (starts with 140, runs perpetually)

Protocol: 2 attempts over 12 months (90 days apart), then Annual Fresh Start

Research: Engagement drops after each failed re-engagement attempt (as low as under 2% on 3rd attempt.

Goal: 18-22% open | 8-10% click
4. Dynamic Newsletter
Bi-weekly Every other Friday

Purpose: Brand awareness + community connection

Innovation: Dynamic content for buyers / sellers (sellers see "Recently Sold," buyers see "New Listings")

Research: 68% of sellers hesitate to list after seeing new inventory (competitive anxiety) (JMR 2023)

Goal: 25-30% open (up from 18.6%)
5. Annual Fresh Start
14 days 3 emails January only

Purpose: Final check-in before permanent archive

Target: Dormant leads who failed 2 reactivation attempts

Why: Life changes = sudden selling need. 0.5-1% annual reactivation rate with minimal effort (HubSpot 2024).

Goal: 1% re-engagement
6. Extended Nurture
365 days Quarterly (4 emails)

Purpose: Match luxury market's 12-18 month decision cycle

Target: Warm leads who completed initial sequences but didn't book

Dual Track: Runs in parallel with bi-weekly newsletter (28 emails/year = 2.3/month = below fatigue threshold)

Goal: 25-30% open | 8-12% click

How leads flow from entry to conversion through behavioral-based routing.

  • Event-specific forms

Websites

  • Google Ads

Step 2: Integration & Auto-Tagging

Lofty Forms Path: Zapier → Mailchimp → Auto-tagged "Source: Website - [Form Type]"

Step 3: Mailchimp Triage (Auto-Routing Based on Source Tag)

New Seller Lead Welcome

IF Source: "JotForm - Event"
Sequence 2

Event Lead Nurture

60 days | 6 emails + 2 SMS

IF Lead Inactive 90+ Days
Sequence 3

Database Reactivation

45 days | 5 emails + 2 SMS

↓

Step 4: After Sequence Completes - Behavioral-Based Routing

Hot Lead

Criteria: Booked appointment or replied "Ready"

Destination: Move to Lofty CRM

  • Danielle's 30-day Action Plan
  • Manual follow-up templates
  • Full CRM context & notes
  • Transaction pipeline
Warm Lead

Criteria: 1+ opens/clicks or Homebot subscription

Destination: Stay in Mailchimp

  • Extended Nurture (Sequence 6)
  • 12 months, quarterly touchpoints
  • + Bi-weekly Newsletter
  • Dual-track nurture approach
Cold Lead

Criteria: 0 opens OR only 1-2 opens

Destination: Stay in Mailchimp

  • Newsletter only (bi-weekly)
  • Maintain brand awareness
  • May re-enter Reactivation if goes dormant again (90+ days inactive)

Transition Strategy: JotForm to Website Forms

Research: On-domain landing pages achieve 34% higher conversion than external forms (Unbounce 2024).

The Principle: Every new campaign is an opportunity to improve lead capture. Over time, create dedicated channel landing pages. For now, stick with what we have (jotform + website / lofty forms) until you’re ready to upgrade this part of your lead capture.

  • Start with: Strategy & Starbucks form migration
